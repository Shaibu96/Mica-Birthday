Her story

Look how far
she has come

From GIS to the world. I watched every single one of these chapters happen in real time, and I am still in awe.

2015

She packed her bags and left for the UK

She left home. Ghana to England, just like that. I remember the feeling of watching her go and knowing, with complete certainty, that she was going to be okay. More than okay. Four years later she graduated, and I was not even a little bit surprised.

2017

She landed IBM while still a student

IBM called and she answered. As a student. Managing services across 200 NHS trusts before she had even finished her degree. I have always known she was built differently. This was just the first time the world got to see it too.

2020

She got her Masters. With Distinction.

She went back to study. A whole Masters. And she came out the other side with a Distinction. I smiled so hard when I found out. I cannot explain it. It is just the most Mica outcome possible. Of course it was a Distinction.

✦ Distinction

2021

She came home and got straight to work

She came home. Back to Accra, back to us. And she hit the ground running. No pause, no in-between. She had always known what she wanted to do with her life, and she was not about to slow down now.

2022

MEST Africa called. She answered.

MEST Africa. Nearly three years. She started as an Associate and left as Lead. But here is what the title does not tell you: the care she put into every single piece of work she did there. She does not just do jobs. She builds things that last.

2025

Now she is doing it on a global stage

And now this. Global. Meaningful. Exactly where she was always heading. I have had the honour of watching every chapter of this story unfold. And I want her to know: I see her. I always have. And I am so deeply, quietly proud.
